What are smart cities?

A smart city is a place where traditional networks and services are made more efficient with the use of digital solutions for the benefit of its inhabitants and business. 


A smart city goes beyond the use of digital technologies for better resource use and less emissions. It means smarter urban transport networks, upgraded water supply and waste disposal facilities and more efficient ways to light and heat buildings. It also means a more interactive and responsive city administration, safer public spaces and meeting the needs of an ageing population.

Current State

According to the World Bank, in 2018, 80% of Spaniards lived in cities and that percentage is expected to reach 88% by 2050. In this new scenario, public and private entities are in collaboration to find different strategies for improving the performance and environmental impact of cities.


Smart cities are an efficient solution that seeks a balance between people, institutions, technology, and sustainability. Through an integrated and holistic approach, smart cities aim to reduce environmental impact, increase economic competitiveness, guarantee security for all citizens, and promote transparency in governance.

Smart Cities: The Future of Urban Living

Core Technologies in Smart Cities

AI and Big Data

Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Big Data are transforming urban management. By using data from city sensors and devices, AI helps make smarter decisions, like improving traffic flow, predicting utility needs, and enhancing public safety by spotting high-risk areas.

Internet of Things (IoT)

The Internet of Things (IoT) is crucial for Smart Cities, connecting devices like sensors, vehicles, and appliances to gather and share data. This enables real-time monitoring and management of city resources, leading to better energy use, timely public transport updates, and quicker emergency responses, making urban life more efficient and convenient.

Cloud Computing

Cloud computing acts as the backbone of Smart Cities, providing the data processing power and storage needed for efficient management. It handles the large volumes of data from IoT devices and AI applications, enabling seamless integration and collaboration between city services. This leads to more coordinated urban management and improved service delivery to residents.

Smart Lighting and Energy Management

Smart lighting and energy management are key to a sustainable Smart City. Technologies like LED lights and automated controls cut energy use and costs. These systems adjust brightness according to time or occupancy, maximising energy efficiency.

Future Possibilities

As we enter a new era of urban development, Smart Cities are leading the way with advanced technologies like Artificial Intelligence (AI), the Internet of Things (IoT), and cloud computing. These innovations enhance efficiency, sustainability, and the quality of life by optimising resource management and improving city operations.

Human Interaction

The concept of Smart Cities now emphasises not just efficiency but also human interactions and inclusivity. Traditional models focus on centralizing data for operations like traffic and waste management, which can create a "black box society" where citizens have little control over their data and limited involvement in decisions. This approach can undermine individual agency and overlook the more human-centric aspects of urban life.

AI and IoT enable real-time data analysis and smarter decision-making, while cloud computing ensures seamless integration across city services. However, progress must be balanced with ethical considerations to protect privacy, inclusivity, and sustainability.

The future of Smart Cities is about blending technological advancement with our core values, creating urban environments that are not only efficient but also inclusive and responsible. By focusing on these principles, we can build cities that truly enhance human experience and reflect our highest aspirations.

Inclusive Data Interaction

Expanding data interactions in Smart Cities now includes considering more-than-human entities such as animals, plants, and insects. This approach decentralises data use from being purely human-centric, fostering a more inclusive and sustainable urban environment by addressing the needs of the entire ecosystem.

Data Negotiability

The focus is on creating transparent and negotiable frameworks for public data. This involves sharing IoT infrastructure responsibly while ensuring compliance with privacy regulations. The goal is to give citizens more control over how their data is used and who benefits from it.

Rural Connectivity and Health

Smart City technologies are being explored to improve health outcomes in rural areas by enhancing connectivity and data sharing. By implementing IoT solutions in low-connectivity regions, these technologies aim to bridge the gap between urban and rural communities, improving health management and overall well-being.
